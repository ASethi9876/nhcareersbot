# Careers Info

## Degree
A degree is achieved once someone has finished their university course. We offer Graduate programmes similar to our apprenticeships too!
the Graduate programmes we offered last year include: Generalist, Digital, Finance, Environmental, Project Management, Civil Engineering, Structural and Electrical Engineering, Systems Engineering and Digital and Technology Solutions. 

## Apprenticeship
Our apprenticeships range in programme and come in different levels. These can be level 3, 4, 5 or 6. You’ll spend most of your time working whilst also studying part-time
Level 3 apprenticeships are also known as advanced apprenticeships and are generally considered to be equivalent to two A level passes. If you want to gain further qualifications, you can go on to complete higher and degree apprenticeships.
Level 4 and 5 apprenticeships are higher apprenticeships which are equivalent to first year or a university degree or a foundation degree. 
Level 6 apprenticeships are a degree equivalent qualification earned while you work. Degree apprenticeships take three to six years to complete, depending on the course level.

## T Level
T Levels are 2-year courses which are taken after GCSEs and are broadly equivalent 3 A Levels. These are agreed between education provider and employer. T Levels are equivalent to three A-Levels with 80% of the student’s time spent in the classroom and 20% within the workplace. 

## BTEC
BTECs combine practical learning with subject and theory content, such as coursework. These can be subject specific that link to your career aspirations.

## A Level
A Levels are subject-based qualifications that can lead to university, further study, training, or work. These can be subject specific that link to your career aspirations.
